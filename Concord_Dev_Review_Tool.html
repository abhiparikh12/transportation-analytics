§18.160.110(A)(2)(a) | City Traffic Study Guidelines
Verify driveway count: max 2 driveways per frontage for multi/non-residential
TRAREQ
Multi-family and nonresidential: no more than 2 driveways on same frontage unless traffic study justifies more. City Engineer approves any exception.
§18.160.110(A)(2)(a)
Access directed to lowest-volume street where multiple streets available
TRAREQ
Where property has access to more than one street, direct access to lowest volume street to minimize traffic impacts. Document in project conditions.
§18.160.110(A)(2)(b)
Driveway setback from corner: minimum 60 ft from nearest intersection
TRAREQ
Measured from centerline of driveway to face of curb of intersecting street. Greater distance may be required by City Engineer for collector/arterial streets.
§18.160.110(B)(1)
Driveway spacing: 28 ft minimum between driveways on same frontage
TRAREQ
SFR: 28 ft separation (shared driveway may be approved by City Engineer). Multi/Nonresidential: 28 ft minimum between centerlines. Exceptions require City Engineer approval.
§18.160.110(B)(2)
Driveway width per Table 18.160.110 — max 30 ft for multi/non-residential
TRAREQ
Verify driveway widths meet minimums per Table 18.160.110. Maximum driveway width is 30 feet, exclusive of median divider width.
§18.160.110(A)(2)(c)
Median strip required for parking areas serving >25 vehicles
TRACOND
Entrance to parking area accommodating more than 25 vehicles shall include a median strip to eliminate cross-traffic within 50 ft of ROW. Design subject to City Engineer approval.
§18.160.110(B)(5)
All parking spaces accessible without re-entering public ROW
TRAREQ
Parking facility design must allow access to all spaces without returning to a public right-of-way. Alleys may be used as maneuvering space. Exception requires UP and physical impossibility finding.
